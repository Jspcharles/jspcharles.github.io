About

Reducing basis risk with explainable AI

I'm a PhD candidate in Information Systems at the University of Southern Queensland. My thesis, Deep Learning the Drought: Explainable AI Approaches for Reducing Basis Risk in Weather Index Insurance for Agribusiness, sits at the intersection of deep learning, drought index modelling (SPI/SPEI), and interpretability methods like SHAP and attention weights.

Before the PhD, I spent several years teaching computer science and leading LMS infrastructure at Sabaragamuwa University of Sri Lanka, followed by applied AI and web-compliance work in industry with Eyeo GmbH in Germany. That mix of classroom, systems, and industry experience now grounds a research practice focused on models that have to be trusted by the people who rely on them — not just accurate on a benchmark.
